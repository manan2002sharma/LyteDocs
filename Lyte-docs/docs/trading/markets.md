# Browsing Markets

Lyte gives you two ways to find markets: **search** and **browse**.

---

## Searching Markets

Type any keyword directly in the chat — no command needed.

Examples:
- `Bitcoin ETF`
- `2024 election`
- `Super Bowl`
- `Fed rate cut`

The bot searches Polymarket's active markets and returns the closest matches. Tap any result to see the market details and available outcomes.

---

## Browsing by Category

1. Tap **Markets** in the main menu
2. Browse by event categories (e.g. Crypto, Politics, Sports, Finance)
3. Tap an event to see all markets within it
4. Select a market to trade

---

## Market Detail View

Each market shows:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Title** | The question being predicted |
| **YES price** | Current probability of YES outcome (e.g. $0.65 = 65% chance) |
| **NO price** | Current probability of NO outcome |
| **End date** | When the market resolves |
| **Volume** | Total USDC traded in this market |

---

## Understanding Prices

Polymarket prices represent **implied probabilities**:

- A YES share at **$0.65** means the market thinks there's a 65% chance the event happens
- If you buy YES at $0.65 and the event happens, each share pays out **$1.00** — a 54% profit
- If the event doesn't happen, shares expire worthless

Prices move between **$0.01 and $0.99** based on market activity.

---

## Limit Orders

For advanced users, Lyte also supports **limit orders** — placing an order at a specific price rather than the current market price.

Limit orders are **Good Till Cancelled (GTC)** and stay open until filled or cancelled.

See your open orders in [Pending Orders](orders.md).
